Impact of Access Levels on Operational Workflow

A comparative overview of DV360 Write-Enabled vs. Read-Only configurations for PYLER solutions.

01

AiD (Brand Safety Solution)

Standard Case (Write-Enabled)

  • AiD analyzes all placements across active YouTube campaigns in real-time.
  • Automated Exclusions: Automatically applies blocks for unsafe inventory to prevent further spend.
  • Enables continuous, real-time protection without manual overhead.

Read-Only Case

  • Continuous sync of campaign and placement data for dashboard visualization.
  • Manual Workflow: Sensitive placements must be downloaded via CSV.
  • Exclusions must be manually applied within the DV360 platform.
02

AiD – Custom Filtering (Brand Sensitivity)

Standard Case (Write-Enabled)

  • Define and proactively block specific content types (e.g., negative brand mentions).
  • Identified placements are automatically connected to campaigns and blocked preemptively.

Read-Only Case

  • Identified content is surfaced in the dashboard for monitoring.
  • Data can be exported for manual exclusion but lacks automated connectivity.
03

AiM (Video-level Contextual Targeting Solution)

Standard Case (Write-Enabled)

  • Pyler directly operates targeting on your behalf.
  • Target contexts (events, themes) are defined upfront, and inventory is actively managed.
  • Ensures ads are delivered on the most relevant inventory to drive stronger SOV.

Read-Only Case

  • Pyler provides curated lists of high-relevance inventory based on defined contexts.
  • Operational Limitation: As execution is manual on the client side, we cannot guarantee delivery pacing or full budget utilization.
Strategic Recommendation: Write-enabled access provides the most end-to-end and automated experience, which is essential for leveraging Peak-Intelligence. In read-only setups, maintaining near real-time control over unsuitable placements is operationally challenging.
